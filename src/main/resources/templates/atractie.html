BRAN CASTLE

Surrounded by the Bucegi Mountains, Bran Castle is one of the most famous medieval fortresses in Eastern Europe, renowned worldwide as “Dracula's Castle.” Built in the 14th century, the castle blends real history with the myth of Vlad the Impaler, offering visitors narrow passageways, royal chambers, and a spectacular panorama over the Rucăr-Bran corridor.

Beyond its legendary connection to Bram Stoker's Dracula, the castle served as a royal residence and played an important role in the defense of the Transylvanian border. Today, it houses a museum showcasing collections of furniture, armor, and historical artifacts. The surrounding area includes picturesque hiking trails and traditional Romanian architecture, making it a perfect destination for cultural and outdoor tourism.

  • Interactive medieval museum
  • Panoramic terrace on the bastion
  • Nearby hiking trails
  • Historical exhibitions and themed events
  • Gift shop and local crafts
